Skeletal Muscle Mass (SSM)

Your skeletal muscles are a huge part of daily function. They are responsible for all types of movement in the body, both voluntary and involuntary. Unlike Lean Body Mass (LBM), and Lean Muscle Mass (LMM), which include cardiac and smooth muscle tissue, SSM is only muscle tissue that can be grown through regular exercise.

The name "skeletal muscle" comes from the fact that they attach to bones via tendons. Visible in the model, the fibers of skeletal muscles have distinct red and white lines called striations. In the average human being, SSM comprises roughly 30% to 40% of total body mass. Like all muscles, caring for your skeletal muscle mass is crucial to maintain your health.
