Skeletal Muscle Mass (SSM)

Skeletal muscle tissue comprises roughly 30% to 40% of your total body mass. It attaches to the bone by tendons, hence their name. Skeletal muscle fibers are crossed with a pattern of fine red and white lines called striations.

Skeletal muscles are responsible for all voluntary movements in the human body. Caring for them is crucial to maintain good bodily health.

Pectoralis Major

Located beneath the breast tissue, your pectoralis major, often called the pec, is your superior most muscle of the anterior chest.

It is responsible for the flexion, adduction, and internal rotation of the arm in the shoulder joint.

The pectoralis major is comprised of two heads, the clavicular head (yellow), and the sternocostal head (green).

Exercises that work the Pectoralis Major:

  • Bench Press
  • Dumbbell Press
  • Incline Fly
  • Push Ups
