Skeletal Muscle Mass (SSM)

Skeletal muscle tissue comprises roughly 30% to 40% of your total body mass. It attaches to the bone by tendons, hence the name. Skeletal muscle fibers are crossed with a regular pattern of fine red and white lines, giving those distinctive lines, called “striations.”

Skeletal muscles are responsible for all voluntary movements in the human body. Caring for them is crucial to maintain good bodily health.

Pectoralis Major

The pectoralis major (from Latin pectus 'breast') is the superior most muscle of the anterior chest. It lies underneath the breast tissue and forms the anterior wall of the axilla.

    If you’re a gym lover, you’ll no doubt know these as the "pecs."
    This muscle is responsible for flexion, adduction, and internal rotation of the arm in the shoulder joint.

      What exercises grow it?

      • Bench Press
      • Dumbbell Press
      • Incline Fly
      • Push Ups
